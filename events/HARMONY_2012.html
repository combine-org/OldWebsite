HARMONY 2012: The Hackathon on Resources for Modeling in Biology

Department of Bioinformatics - BiGCaT
Maastricht, The Netherlands
May 21–25, 2012

In 2010, the systems biology standards communities inaugurated a new, broader series of meetings to replace, among other events, the individual SBML and SBGN workshops and BioPAX meetings.

HARMONY is a hackathon-type meeting, with a focus on development of the standards, interoperability and infrastructure. There is generally not many general discussions or oral presentations during HARMONY; instead, the time is devoted to allowing hands-on hacking and interaction between people focused on practical development of software and standards. The HARMONY 2012 meeting will be hosted by the Department of Bioinformatics from Maastricht University.

There is no conference fee, thanks to all the sponsors.

Travel Information

International airports

  • Brussels airport (BRU): travel time to Maastricht is ~1.5hr by train
  • Amsterdam airport (AMS): travel time to Maastricht is ~2.5hrs by train
  • Duesseldorf airport (DUS): travel time to Maastricht is ~ 2.5hrs by train

Smaller airports (flights within Europe)

  • Maastricht airport (MST): travel time to Maastricht is ~15 minutes by bus
  • Eindhoven airport (EIN): travel time to Maastricht is ~1hr by train

How to get to Maastricht from the different airports?

  • Brussels: take airport train to Brussels Centraal (http://www.brusselsairport.be/en/passngr/to_from_brussels_airport/train/), check the train company's website (http://www.ns.nl/en/travellers/home) for trains from Brussels Centraal to Maastricht.
  • Amsterdam: check the train company's website (http://www.ns.nl/en/travellers/home) for trains from Schiphol (airport) to Maastricht.
  • Duesseldorf: check the German train company's website (http://www.deutschebahn.com/en/start.html) and search for trains from Duesseldorf Airport to Aachen. From Aachen you can take the bus line 50 to Maastricht (takes around 1hr).
  • There are several companies providing shuttle services. If you are arriving with other participants, it might make sense to look into a taxi shuttle (e.g. http://www.easyairporttaxis.com/, http://www.taxi2airport.com/)


Tip for travelers coming from Germany
If you are coming by train, there is direct bus connection (bus 50) from Aachen train station to Maastricht Station (1 hr trip). Please check the bus information here (in German).
