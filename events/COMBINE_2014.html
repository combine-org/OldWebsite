COMBINE 2014

Dates: August 18-22, 2014
Location: Los Angeles, California, USA
Hosted by: University of Southern California

The "Computational Modeling in Biology" Network (COMBINE) is an initiative to coordinate the development of the various community standards and formats in systems biology and related fields.

COMBINE is a workshop-style event with oral presentation, posters, and breakout sessions. The five meeting days will include talks about the COMBINE standards and associated or related standardization efforts, as well as presentations of tools using these standards. Oral presentations will be selected from the submitted abstracts. In addition to oral presentations, poster sessions will allow people to inform each other about their software and other projects in a setting that fosters interaction and in-depth discussion.

The 2014 COMBINE meeting was held at Keck School of Medicine of USC in Los Angeles, California from Monday August 18 to Friday August 22, 2014.

Travel Information

Flying to Los Angeles

The closest international airport to USC is Los Angeles International Airport (LAX). This is likely to be the best option for most international travelers, and is a good option for domestic travelers as well.

A second international airport is John Wayne Airport (SNA), also know as Santa Ana Airport. This airport is smaller and easier to get in and out of, but it is farther away than LAX. However, if you are planning on renting a car anyway, then SNA may be an option for you, especially since flights and car rentals are often less expensive from SNA than LAX. The tradeoff is time: not only is SNA farther away under the best traffic conditions, but during peak travel times, the main highway (I5) between SNA and USC is extremely clogged with traffic. You are better off avoiding the roads completely in the time ranges 07:00–09:00 and again 15:00–20:00. Also, beware that the highways immediately around SNA are particularly convoluted and difficult to navigate. Make sure to study the highways and plan your route ahead of time.

For domestic flights, a third airport to consider is Burbank Airport (BUR). It is the closest airport to USC, but serves only a limited number of airlines. (If you fly Southwest Airlines, however, it is probably your best bet.)
